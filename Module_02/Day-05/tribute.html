The USS Midway

The longest serving aircraft carrier in the 20th century for the US Navy.

Long Service Record

The USS Midway served from 1946 to 1992 all the way from the Vietnam war to the first Iraq conflict. During its lifetime the ship had joined the Seventh fleet multiple times where it had many important firsts.

A more in depth service record.

A History of Firsts

  • First carrier to operate in the sub-artic
  • First and only succesful launch of a German V-2 rocket from a ship
  • First autopilot landing
  • First MIG shot down by pilots during Vietnam war
  • First carrier homeported in a foreign country
  • First Naval ship musuem to recieve one million visitors annualy

Retrofitting/Modernizations

The Midway went through a total of three retrofits through its service history. The first was in 1955 which included upgrading equipment and expanding the flight deck. The second was in 1966 which included an expansion of the flight deck again, and the third and final was in 1986.
